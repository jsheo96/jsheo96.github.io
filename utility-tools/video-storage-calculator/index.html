Example

Four cameras recording continuously at 8 Mbps for 30 days require about 3.46 TB before overhead. With a 10% safety margin, plan for roughly 3.80 TB of usable storage.

What bitrate should I enter?

Use the average encoded bitrate from the camera, NVR, or encoder. H.264 and H.265 streams vary with motion, image complexity, frame rate, and quality settings.

Why add overhead?

Real systems need free space for filesystem overhead, database files, variable bitrate spikes, indexing, thumbnails, and operational headroom. A 10–20% margin is a practical starting point.

FAQ

Does resolution alone determine storage?

No. Storage is driven by bitrate. Two 4K cameras can use very different storage depending on codec, frame rate, quality, and scene complexity.

Is TB decimal or binary?

This calculator shows decimal storage units, where 1 TB equals 1,000 GB. Some operating systems may display a smaller binary-based value.
