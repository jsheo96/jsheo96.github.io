Formula

Bandwidth = width × height × frames per second × bits per pixel × camera count × overhead

Example

A 1920×1080 RGB camera at 60 FPS produces about 2.99 Gbps of raw image data. With 10% overhead, the practical requirement is about 3.28 Gbps, so a 10 Gigabit Ethernet connection is the sensible choice.

How much overhead should I use?

Use 10% for a simple estimate. Use 20–30% when you want extra safety margin for packet overhead, bursts, imperfect utilization, and multiple devices sharing the same uplink.

Does GigE Vision require the full raw bandwidth?

For uncompressed image streams, the required throughput is close to the raw image data rate plus transport overhead. Packet size, inter-packet delay, NIC performance, and switch backplane capacity also affect real-world performance.

FAQ

Can a 1 Gbps link carry a 1920×1080 60 FPS RGB stream?

No. Uncompressed 24-bit RGB at that resolution and frame rate requires roughly 3 Gbps before overhead.

What if the camera uses Bayer 8-bit output?

Select 8 bits per pixel. Bayer output is typically much smaller than 24-bit RGB because demosaicing can occur after transfer.

Should I calculate each camera separately?

You can enter the number of cameras directly. For switch design, compare the total result with the uplink capacity and also verify each individual port speed.
